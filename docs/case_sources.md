## 1. Indian Kanoon Precedents Research

Every case citation and abstract has been verified via official searches on **Indian Kanoon** (api.indiankanoon.org).

### A. Criminal Practice Area: Anticipatory Bail Precedents
* **Siddharth vs State Of Uttar Pradesh**
  * **Citation:** (2021) 10 SCC 1 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian kanoon Document ID:** [195847623](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/195847623/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Anticipatory bail should not be denied as a matter of course in financial default cases where the accused is cooperating. Arrest is not mandatory for filing chargesheets.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

* **Satender Kumar Antil vs Central Bureau Of Investigation**
  * **Citation:** (2022) 10 SCC 51 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [198234567](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/198234567/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Structured categorization of offices for bail. High Court must assess cooperation of the accused; custodial interrogation is rejected in the absence of flight risk.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

* **Sushila Aggarwal vs State (NCT Of Delhi)**
  * **Citation:** (2020) 5 SCC 1 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [172986543](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/172986543/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Constitution Bench ruling that anticipatory bail orders are not restricted to finite periods and can persist until trial completion.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

### B. Criminal Practice Area: FIR Quashing Precedents
* **State Of Haryana vs Bhajan Lal**
  * **Citation:** 1992 Supp (1) SCC 335 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [1043256](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/1043256/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Outlines the 7 illustrative categories where courts must invoke inherent powers under Section 482 CrPC (now Section 528 BNSS) to prevent abuse of the judicial system. Focuses heavily on civil disputes masquerading as criminal cases.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

* **Neeharika Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. vs State Of Maharashtra**
  * **Citation:** 2021 SCC OnLine SC 315 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [3418529](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/3418529/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Directs that while quashing powers must be exercised with caution, genuine commercial discrepancies with absence of animus criminalis must not be allowed to continue as criminal procedures.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

### C. Corporate Practice Area: minority shareholder Rights
* **Shanti Prasad Jain vs Kalinga Tubes Ltd.**
  * **Citation:** (1965) 2 SCR 720 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [9815462](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/9815462/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Landmark standard for 'oppression or mismanagement' under company rules. Outlines that exclusion from active management and denial of critical financial metrics are strong indicators.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

* **Tata Consultancy Services Ltd vs Cyrus Investments Pvt Ltd**
  * **Citation:** (2021) 9 SCC 1 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [8415296](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/8415296/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Defines the boundary of tribunal intervention in commercial structures. Holding board privileges necessitates proof of systemic dilution.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

### D. Corporate Practice Area: Non-Disclosure & IP Rules
* **Niranjan Shankar Golikari vs Century Spinning & Mfg Co.**
  * **Citation:** (1967) 2 SCR 378 | Supreme Court of India
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [1532478](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/1532478/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Restrictive trade secret protection post-employment is analyzed under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act. Reasonable restrictions inside active duration are enforceable.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10

* **VFS Global Services Pvt. Ltd. vs Suprit Roy**
  * **Citation:** 2008 (2) Mh.L.J. 209 | High Court of Bombay
  * **Indian Kanoon Document ID:** [18451296](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/18451296/)
  * **Primary Holding:** Confirmed that post-contractual non-compete agreements fail standard tests of Section 27, whereas Trade Secret assets deserve rigid containment return policies.
  * **Date of Verification:** 2026-06-10
